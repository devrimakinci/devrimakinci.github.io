Who Is Bjarne Stroustrup?

Figure 1: Stroustrup is giving seminar in India [3]

Bjarne Stroustrup was born 30 December 1950. He is a Danish computer scientist. Bjarne Stroustrup graduated from Aarhus University in 1975. He also took doctorate in computer science from Cambridge University in 1979.Stroustrup is the creator of programming language C++ and is one of the pioneers of object oriented programming.[1] Stroustrup was in charge of AT&T Bell Labs’ Large-Scale Programming Research department until 2002. He was member of the Academy of Engineering. He was a fellow of IEEE in 2005. He was a Fellow of the Computer History Museum for his invention of the C++ programming language in 2015. He works as a senior research professor department of computer science at Texas A&M University.[2]

His Contribution To Computer Science

Stroustrup began working up C++ in 1978. He performed works for standardisation of C++ and he carried on works on this subject. He also wrote a book for programming language, The C++ Programming Language. This book is an introduction to C++. Therefore, one of the most preferred book is in programming language. His research interests are distributed systems, software design, programming methods, software development tools and programming languages.[4]

Stroustrup has written many books. Some of the most important books are listed below.

-The C++ Programming Language
-The Design and Evolution of C++
-Programming: Principles and Practice Using C++
-The Annotated C++ Reference Manual


His Awards

Stroustrup also has won a lot of awards. Some of the notable awards are listed below.

-Grace Murray Hopper Award (1993)
-IEEE Computer Society Computer Entrepreneur Award (2004)
-William Procter Prize for Scientific Achievement (2005)
-Dr. Doob’s Excellence in Programming Award (2008)
